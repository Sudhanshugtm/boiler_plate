## Physical Dimensions & Size

### Atomic Radius
- **General size**: Typically around 100 picometers (pm) across
- **Smallest atom (Helium)**: 32 pm radius
- **One of the largest (Caesium)**: 225 pm radius
- **Diameter range**: 62 pm (Helium) to 520 pm (Caesium)

### Comparative Measurements
- **Human hair width**: About 1 million carbon atoms wide
- **Water droplet**: Contains about 2 sextillion (2 × 10²¹) atoms of oxygen, and twice that number of hydrogen atoms
- **Diamond carat**: A single carat (2 × 10⁻⁴ kg) contains about 10²² atoms of carbon
- **One angstrom**: 10⁻¹⁰ m or 100 pm (shown in helium atom illustration)

## Mass & Weight

### Particle Masses
- **Electron mass**: 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg
- **Proton mass**: 1.6726 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
- **Neutron mass**: 1.6749 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
- **Atom mass range**: 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ to 4.52 × 10⁻²⁵ kg

### Specific Isotopes
- **Carbon-12**: Approximately 1.66 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
- **Hydrogen-1**: 1.007825 Da (daltons)
- **Lead-208** (heaviest stable atom): 207.9766521 Da

### Mass Distribution
- **More than 99.94%** of an atom's mass is in the nucleus

## Historical Measurements & Ratios

### Dalton's Law Examples

#### Tin Oxide Ratios
- Grey powder: 88.1% tin, 11.9% oxygen
- White powder: 78.7% tin, 21.3% oxygen
- Oxygen per 100g tin: 13.5g vs 27g (ratio 1:2)

#### Iron Oxide Ratios
- Black powder: 78.1% iron, 21.9% oxygen
- Red powder: 70.4% iron, 29.6% oxygen
- Oxygen per 100g iron: 28g vs 42g (ratio 2:3)

#### Nitrogen Oxide Ratios
- Nitrous oxide: 63.3% nitrogen, 36.7% oxygen
- Nitric oxide: 44.05% nitrogen, 55.95% oxygen
- Nitrogen dioxide: 29.5% nitrogen, 70.5% oxygen
- Oxygen per 140g nitrogen: 80g, 160g, 320g (ratio 1:2:4)

- Electrons measured as **1,700 times lighter** than hydrogen atom
- Electrons are **1/1700** the mass of hydrogen

## Nuclear & Quantum Properties

### Nucleon Dimensions
- **Proton and neutron size**: On the order of 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁵ m (2.5 femtometers)

### Nuclear Radius Formula
- Radius ≈ 1.07 × ∛A femtometers (where A = total nucleons)

### Nuclear Force Range
- **Strong force range**: Effective at distances smaller than 2.5 fm
- **Radius ratio**: Nucleus is on the order of 10⁵ fm compared to atom radius

### Binding Energy
- **Hydrogen ionization**: Requires only 13.6 eV to strip ground-state electron
- **Deuterium nucleus splitting**: Requires 2.23 million eV

### Solar Core Fusion
- **Proton fusion energy requirement**: 3 to 10 keV to overcome Coulomb barrier

## Isotopes & Elements

### Element Statistics
- **Total chemical elements**: Up to 118 (from hydrogen to oganesson)
- **Naturally occurring nuclides on Earth**: About 339
- **Stable isotopes**: 251 nuclides have not been observed to decay
- **Theoretically stable**: Only 90 nuclides
- **Additional stable**: 161 more (total 251) not observed to decay
- **Long-lived radioactive**: 35 nuclides with half-lives longer than 100 million years
- **Primordial nuclides**: 286 nuclides total (251 stable + 35 long-lived)
- **Short-lived natural**: 53 additional nuclides occur naturally

### Stable Isotope Statistics
- **Elements with stable isotopes**: 80 chemical elements
- **Average stable isotopes per element**: 3.1
- **Monoisotopic elements**: 26 elements have only one stable isotope
- **Maximum stable isotopes**: 10 (for tin)
- **Elements with no stable isotopes**: Technetium (43), Promethium (61), and all ≥83

### Specific Isotopes
- **Odd-odd stable nuclides**: Only 4 (hydrogen-2/deuterium, lithium-6, boron-10, nitrogen-14)
- **Radioactive odd-odd with billion+ year half-life**: 4 (potassium-40, vanadium-50, lanthanum-138, lutetium-176)
- **Magic numbers**: Filled quantum shells (e.g., 50 protons for tin)

## Universal Scale

### Observable Universe
- **Baryonic matter**: Forms about 4% of total energy density
- **Average density**: About 0.25 particles/m³ (mostly protons and electrons)

### Galactic Scale
- **Interstellar medium density**: 10⁵ to 10⁹ atoms/m³
- **Solar neighborhood density**: Only about 10³ atoms/m³
- **Milky Way baryonic matter**: Up to 95% concentrated inside stars
- **Baryonic mass of galaxy**: About 10% of total galaxy mass

### Earth-Specific
- **Total atoms on Earth**: Approximately 1.33 × 10⁵⁰ atoms
- **Atmospheric composition**: 99% bound in molecules, only small amounts as independent noble gas atoms
- **Helium in Earth's crust**: About 99% is from alpha decay

### Big Bang Timeline
- **Recombination epoch**: 380,000 years after Big Bang (when electrons attached to nuclei)
- **Big Bang nucleosynthesis**: Occurred in about 3 minutes

## Chemical Elements

### Specific Element Numbers
- **Sodium**: Any atom with 11 protons
- **Copper**: Any atom with 29 protons
- **Iron atomic numbers**: Mentioned as up to 26 for exothermic fusion
- **Nickel nucleon number**: About 60 total nucleons
- **Bismuth (83)**: Radioactivity so slight as to be practically negligible

## Quantum & Energy States

### Avogadro's Number
- **Atoms per mole**: About 6.022 × 10²³
- **Carbon-12 mole**: Exactly 0.012 kg (12 grams)

### Scattering & Deflection
- **Rutherford experiments**: Small number deflected by angles greater than 90°
